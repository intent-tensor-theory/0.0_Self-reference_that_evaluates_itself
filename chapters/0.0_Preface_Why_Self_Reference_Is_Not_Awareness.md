# 章零・零 (Chapter 0.0 / 7.0)

## Preface — Why Self-Reference Is Not Awareness

**Progress: 14.3%**

---

「自分に戻るものは多い。だが、自分を測るものは少ない。」  
*Many things return to themselves. Few evaluate themselves.*

---

### 0.0.1 The Second Silence

After space is earned, a new silence appears.

It is tempting to fill that silence with familiar words:  
*observer, mind, consciousness, self.*

This book refuses that temptation.

**Self-reference is common.**  
**Awareness is rare.**

Confusing the two has stalled progress across physics, computation, philosophy, and cognitive science for decades.

This text begins by separating them cleanly.

---

### 0.0.2 Why Recursion Is Not Enough

The previous volume demonstrated how recursion can:

- generate structure,
- store memory,
- lock persistence,
- permit coexistence.

**None of that produces a self.**

A recursive system may:

- loop indefinitely,
- stabilize indefinitely,
- even model its own behavior—

and still have no internal point of view.

> Recursion explains how things persist.  
> It does not explain why anything matters.

That gap is the subject of this book.

---

### 0.0.3 The Common Error

Across disciplines, a single mistake repeats under different names:

- In physics: "the universe observes itself."
- In computation: "feedback implies intelligence."
- In philosophy: "self-reference implies consciousness."
- In AI: "complexity produces awareness."

All of these commit the same error:

> **They treat self-reference as evaluation.**

This is false.

A mirror refers to itself.  
A thermostat feeds back.  
A fractal reproduces its own pattern.

**None of these evaluate themselves.**

---

### 0.0.4 What "Evaluation" Means Here

Evaluation is not measurement.  
It is not optimization.  
It is not reward.

Evaluation is defined narrowly:

> **Evaluation is the internal comparison of states according to a system-generated preference.**

This definition contains three constraints:

1. Comparison must be **internal**
2. Preference must be **endogenous**
3. The result must **alter future admissibility**

Without all three, there is no evaluation.

Without evaluation, there is no self.

---

### 0.0.5 Why Awareness Is Not the Starting Point

Awareness is phenomenological.  
This book is structural.

Phenomenology describes *what it is like.*  
Structure describes *what must exist* for "likeness" to arise at all.

Attempting to derive structure from experience reverses causality.

Therefore:

> This book does not attempt to explain experience.  
> It explains the conditions under which experience could later occur.

Awareness belongs to a later inquiry.

---

### 0.0.6 What This Book Will Not Do

This text will not:

- define consciousness,
- explain qualia,
- solve the hard problem,
- argue for or against free will,
- anthropomorphize systems,
- invoke observers as primitives.

Those moves end inquiry.  
This book begins before them.

---

### 0.0.7 The Central Question

The central question is precise:

> **What must be true for a system to evaluate its own state as better or worse, rather than merely different?**

Not *what does it feel like.*  
Not *how complex is it.*  
Not *how intelligent is it.*

Only: **what structural conditions make evaluation possible at all?**

---

### 0.0.8 Why This Is Orthogonal to Space

The previous text derived space from coexistence.

This text does not modify that result.

Evaluation does not require space to be fundamental.  
It requires space to be available.

Thus, this book proceeds **after** space, but not **above** it.

The two ascents are independent.

---

### 0.0.9 The Discipline Ahead

As before, restraint is essential.

We will forbid, until earned:

- subject
- meaning
- intention
- goal
- value
- choice

If these appear too early, the derivation fails.

---

### 0.0.10 How This Book Ends

This book will end when the following can be stated precisely:

- what self-reference actually is,
- what evaluation requires,
- why agency is structural, not mystical,
- and where formal explanation must stop.

It will not end with consciousness.

That is deliberate.

---

### 0.0.11 A Warning to the Reader

If you are searching for reassurance that minds are special, you will not find it here.

If you are searching for proof that machines cannot think, you will not find it here.

If you are searching for a clean boundary between:

- feedback and self,
- recursion and meaning,
- persistence and agency—

you are in the right place.

---

「自己は名前ではない。条件である。」  
*The self is not a name. It is a condition.*
